About Blood Donation

Types of Blood8 Donation

There are several types of blood donations, including:

  • Whole Blood Donation: The most common type, where about a pint of blood is collected.
  • Platelet Donation: Platelets are separated from other blood components and collected.
  • Plasma Donation: Plasma is collected for patients with clotting disorders.

Eligibility Criteria

To donate blood, you must:

  • Be at least 18 years old (up to 65).
  • Weigh at least 50 kg.
  • Be in good health with no recent surgeries or illnesses.
  • Not have donated blood in the last 3 months (for whole blood).

Benefits of Blood Donation

Donating blood:

  • Saves lives by providing blood for surgeries, accidents, and medical treatments.
  • Helps maintain your own health by stimulating new blood cell production.
  • Provides a free health check-up during the donation process.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Is blood donation safe?

A: Yes, it’s safe when done at certified centers with sterile equipment.

Q: How long does it take?

A: The process takes about 45 minutes, including registration and rest.

Q: Can I donate if I have a tattoo?

A: You can donate after 6 months from getting a tattoo, provided it was done at a licensed facility.
